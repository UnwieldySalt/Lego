About Us

Our Origin


The Lego company was founded in 1932 by Ole Kirk Christiansen, a master carpenter from Billund in Denmark. The first Lego blocks were introduced in 1949 and have since become a staple in children's toy collections worldwide. As the company grew, Godtfred Kirk Christiansen took over the reins and introduced the Lego System of Play in 1955, which allowed for the creation of more complex structures. We have since expanded our product line to include a wide range of toys, games, and theme parks. Today, Lego is one of the most successful game brands in marketing history and continues to inspire creativity and imagination in both children and adults alike.


Our Goal


At Lego, our goal is to inspire and develop the builders of tomorrow through the power of play. We believe that children should be given the freedom to explore their creativity and imagination, and we strive to provide them with the tools to do so. Our core values include imagination, creativity, fun, learning, and caring. We believe that these values are essential for children's development and growth, and we aim to incorporate them into everything we do.


Future Of LEGO


At LEGO, we thrive to make the company what Ole wanted it to be. Ole aspired to make LEGO seem limitless to meet children's creativity. As we move forward, we respect Ole's dream here at LEGO and aim to expand the boundaries of human imagination.
